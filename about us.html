What is a Speakeasy Bar?

A speakeasy bar is an establishment that illegally served alcoholic beverages during the Prohibition era in the United States (1920-1933). These hidden bars were often concealed behind unmarked doors or disguised as legitimate businesses.

Originating from the need to bypass the 18th Amendment, speakeasies became a cultural phenomenon, fostering a sense of rebellion and secretive social gatherings. Today, many modern bars adopt the speakeasy aesthetic, offering an intimate atmosphere with craft cocktails and a nod to the rich history of the Prohibition era.
